Get to know us

She/Her is an organisation dedicated towards women who have been the heroes of their own lives and paved their own paths to get themselves and their people a better and respectful life.The Stories and achievements of these women will truly inspire other women and especially the young girls of our country.

We at She/Her also try our level best to provide the best of resources and facilities to empower and educate the women and girls who unfortunately could not afford to do so by themselves. The strength that these women held in order to come out of their circumstances despite all odds was enough motivating for us to dedicate an organisation like this to celebrate them and present them as role models for all the other women out there.

Stories that inspire millions

Nauroti Devi

A Dalit by birth, Naurotiâ€™s journey from a stone-cutter to a computer-educated Sarpanch is remarkable in itself. Nauroti was in Harmada village in Kishangarh Tehsil in Ajmer district of Rajasthan. She never attended school nor receive any formal degree. However, she began a new chapter in her life when she decided join a six-month-long literacy training programme. With her quick learning skills, she gained everything a leader requires in his/her public life and made to the election to the Sarpanch of Harmada in 2010. As a Sarpanch, Nauroti in her five-year stint, dedicated herself completely to promoting development in the region and fought against the alcohol mafia and worked tirelessly for the construction of water bodies, toilets and houses and other facilities.


Dalimi Patgiri

Dalmi Patgiri, who hails from interior Assam, overcame the hurdles of poverty to prove her merit. Once a poor housewife, she broke through the shackles of poverty by making utensils that use arecanut sheets for raw materials, thus creating a success story that inspired many others to take risk in their life and lead an independent life.


Navalben Dalsangbhai Chaudhary

Hailing from the village of Nagana in the district of Banaskantha, 62-year-old Navalben Dalsangbhai Chaudhary defied all odds to cause a mini-revolution in her district. According to reports, she made a record by selling milk worth Rs 1.10 crore in 2020, earning a profit of Rs 3.50 lakh per month. In 2019, she sold Rs 87.95 lakh worth of milk. Navalben started a milk company at her home in 2020. Now has over 80 buffaloes and 45 cows that cater to the milk needs of people in several villages.

Help and support we offer

At She/Her, we are dedicated to advancing the cause of women's literacy, empowerment, and education. Our mission is rooted in the belief that when women are educated and empowered, they have the potential to transform not only their own lives but also their communities and the world at large.

Our Vision

Our vision is to create a world where every woman, regardless of her background or circumstances, has the opportunity to access quality education, develop essential life skills, and become a confident and self-reliant individual.


Our Mission

Our mission is to empower women through education by providing them with the tools, resources, and support they need to thrive academically, professionally, and personally.


Our Approach

At She/Her, we take a holistic approach to women's education and empowerment. Our programs are designed to address the diverse needs and challenges that women face.
